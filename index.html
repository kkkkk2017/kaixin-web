Hi, I'm
Kaixin Ji (吉凯昕)

"Decision-Making, Quantifying, Interaction"

PhD Candidate (Data Science), RMIT & ADM+S (ARC Centre of Excellence for Automated Decision-Making and Society)

Keywords: Cognitive Bias, Interactive Information Retrieval, Wearable Sensors, User Study

My research is about quantifying and measuring users' cognitive bias in interactive information-seeking (IIR) with multi-modal physiological sensing. I look at cognitive bias as a deviation in decision-making when selecting or judging information. The physiological data include brain activity (EEG), electrodermal activity (EDA), eye, motion, etc. I enjoy conducting human-centered research to translate abstract concepts into tangible solutions, using multidisciplinary knowledge.
Outside of academia, I also like to explore various hobbies, including cooking, photography, vlogs, badminton, golf, and figure skating. Besides, I play the Chinese Harp (Guzheng) and now learning Guqing.
